Target's AI figured out a teenager was pregnant before she told her own dad.

Then it mailed baby coupons to her house. 😬

That's not a Black Mirror episode—it happened.

And it's the perfect example of why #AIethics in business is so complicated right now.

The same systems we're using to "optimize" are making decisions about people's bodies, job applications, and medical care... often without anyone checking if those decisions are actually fair.

Amazon built a recruiting AI that penalized any resume with the word "women's" on it. Women's chess club? Filtered out. Women's basketball captain? Never seen by a human.

The algorithm learned bias from historical data and scaled it at machine speed.

Here's the uncomfortable question nobody's asking:

What's YOUR company's AI doing that you haven't audited yet?

The gap between "efficient" and "ethical" is where reputations go to die.
