Real talk: 80% of AI projects fail before delivering any value.

Not because the technology doesn't work—but because companies solve the wrong problems.

Watched a client spend $300K on a "perfect" machine learning system. It predicted equipment failures flawlessly. 

The implementation? Total disaster.

No clean data. Team couldn't act on predictions. Expensive shelfware. 💸

After 50+ implementations, the pattern is clear:

Before ANY AI project, ask 3 questions:

→ Does this solve a $100K+ problem?
→ Do we actually have the data?
→ Will the team use it?

If you can't answer yes to all three, you're not ready.

Start simpler: find your most expensive manual process that involves data. That's probably your AI starting point.
