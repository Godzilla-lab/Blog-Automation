### The 5-Component Automation System

Here's the exact tech stack that works for 100-300 unit operations.

#### Component 1: Tenant Self-Service Portal

A web and mobile app where tenants submit requests 24/7.

**The smart form asks:**
- What's the issue? *(dropdown: plumbing, electrical, HVAC, appliance)*
- Which room? *(dropdown specific to their unit)*
- How urgent? *(emergency, urgent, routine)*
- Describe the problem *(text field with minimum character requirement)*
- Photo upload *(drag-and-drop or camera)*

**Immediate confirmation:**
> "Request received! Ticket #12345 created. You'll hear from us within 2 hours (urgent) or 24 hours (routine)."

**Status tracking:**
Tenants log in anytime to see: *"Assigned to ABC Plumbing—scheduled for Tuesday 2-4pm."*

No phone calls needed. No wondering if anyone got the message.

**Result: 85% of requests come through the portal with complete, structured information.**

#### Component 2: Smart Triage and Auto-Routing

The system automatically assigns priority:

| Request Type | Priority | Action |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| No heat in winter | Emergency | Auto-escalate to property manager immediately |
| Leaky faucet | Urgent | Route to plumber, schedule within 48 hours |
| Cabinet door loose | Routine | Batch with non-urgent requests, schedule within 7 days |

Auto-assignment based on specialty and availability:
- Plumbing requests → plumber with soonest availability
- HVAC requests → HVAC contractor
- After-hours emergencies → on-call contractor

**No manual sorting. No phone tag to figure out who handles what.**

#### Component 3: Contractor Coordination Automation

Contractors receive automated notifications with full details:

> **New Maintenance Request**
> 
> Property: 123 Main St, Unit 5B
> Issue: Kitchen sink leaking under cabinet
> Priority: Urgent
> Photos: [attached]
> 
> [Click here to schedule]

The contractor clicks the scheduling link, selects an available time slot.

**Tenant gets auto-notification:**
> "ABC Plumbing scheduled for Thursday, March 15, 2-4pm. Access code: 5678."

**24-hour reminder to both parties.**

**Result: 70% reduction in coordination time. Requests go from submission to scheduled in under 10 minutes.**

#### Component 4: Automated Follow-Up and Completion

After the contractor marks the job complete:

**Tenant receives auto-survey:**
> "Was the issue resolved? Rate your experience 1-5 stars."

If tenant reports NOT resolved → auto-escalates back to property manager.

If confirmed → ticket auto-closes, generates work order record.

**All data logged automatically. No manual entry.**

**Result: 90% completion tracking accuracy (vs. 60% with manual systems).**

#### Component 5: Preventive Maintenance Automation

Scheduled recurring maintenance auto-generated:
- HVAC filter changes every 90 days
- Pest control monthly
- Smoke detector checks annually

The system creates requests, assigns contractors, schedules, and tracks completion.

Property managers get alerts if preventive maintenance is overdue.

**Result: 60% reduction in emergency repairs because small issues get caught during preventive checks.**

## The 8-Week Implementation Roadmap

Here's exactly how to implement maintenance automation without disrupting operations.

This roadmap is proven across 50+ property management deployments.

### Week 1: Audit Your Current Process

**Track everything for 7 days:**
- How many requests come in?
- Where do they come from? (text, email, call, etc.)
- How long does each take to coordinate?
- Where do requests get lost?

**Calculate your baseline:**
- Average time from request to completion
- Number of touchpoints per request
- Current tenant satisfaction (if tracked)

**Identify top pain points:**
- Communication gaps
- Contractor coordination failures
- Follow-up that falls through cracks

**Select your software:**

| Platform Type | Examples | Best For |
|--------------|----------|----------|
| Full PM software | Buildium, AppFolio, Propertyware | 100+ units needing all-in-one |
| Standalone maintenance | ServiceChannel, Lula | Adding to existing systems |
| Custom automation | Hex AI Agency solutions | Complex workflows or integrations |

**Must-haves:**
- Tenant portal (web + mobile)
- Contractor portal
- Automated notifications
- Status tracking
- Reporting dashboard

### Week 2-3: System Configuration

**Set up tenant portal:**
- Property-specific fields (unit layouts, common areas)
- Customized issue categories

**Upload data:**
- Tenant contact info (email + phone for SMS)
- Contractor info (specialties, service areas, contact)

**Create automation rules:**
- Request categories and priority assignments
- Emergency vs. routine definitions
- Notification templates

**Test internally:**
Run 2-3 fake requests through the entire workflow.

### Week 3-4: Contractor Onboarding

**Introduce the system to your top 3-5 contractors.**

Explain the benefits for them:
- Clear work orders with photos
- Self-scheduling (no phone tag)
- Faster payment processing

**Train them on the contractor portal:**
- Receiving requests
- Scheduling appointments
- Marking completion
- Submitting invoices

**Test with real requests.** Get feedback. Adjust workflow.

**Incentivize adoption:**
> "Use the portal for 30 days, and we'll prioritize your invoices for faster payment."

### Week 4-5: Tenant Communication Rollout

**Announce the new system to all tenants.**

Email + physical letter:
> "Exciting update! Submit maintenance requests 24/7 via our new tenant portal. Faster responses, better tracking, no phone tag."

**Emphasize THEIR benefits:**
- Submit anytime, day or night
- Include photos so contractors come prepared
- Track status without calling

**Provide simple instructions:**
> Visit [portal URL] → Log in with your email → Click "Submit Request" → Done!

**Offer support:**
> "Questions? Call us during business hours and we'll walk you through it."

### Week 5-6: Soft Launch

**Run parallel systems for two weeks.**

Still accept texts, emails, and calls—but encourage portal use.

When tenant texts:
> "Thanks! I've logged this in our system. For faster service next time, submit via [portal link]—you can track status 24/7."

**Track adoption metrics:**
- What percentage coming through portal vs. old channels?
- Where are tenants getting stuck?

**Provide extra support.** Quick response times. Troubleshooting help.

### Week 7: Full Launch

**Announce portal is now the primary method:**
> "Effective [date], all maintenance requests should be submitted via tenant portal for fastest response."

**Update all touchpoints:**
- Voicemail: "For maintenance requests, visit [portal URL]."
- Email auto-responders: direct to portal
- Website: prominent portal link

**Still accept emergency calls.** But routine requests go through the portal.

### Week 8+: Optimization

**Review first-month data:**
- Adoption rate (target: 75%+ by end of month 2)
- Time savings vs. baseline
- Tenant satisfaction feedback

**Adjust workflows:**
- Refine categories based on actual usage
- Update priority rules if needed
- Improve notification wording

**Double down on wins.** Share success stories with tenants and contractors.
