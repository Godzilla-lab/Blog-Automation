Create a high-converting LinkedIn post following these proven 2026 best practices:

**CRITICAL - THE HOOK (First 2 Lines):**
- These appear before "see more" - make or break the post
- Use pattern interrupts: contrarian takes, shocking stats, bold questions
- Examples: "Most businesses are using AI wrong..." / "We saved a client $50K with one AI tool..." / "The AI tools everyone recommends? Overrated."
- NO generic openings like "AI is transforming business..."
- Create curiosity or controversy

**LENGTH & STRUCTURE:**
- Sweet spot: 1300-1900 characters (LinkedIn algorithm loves this)
- Use line breaks every 1-2 sentences for mobile readability
- Short paragraphs = higher engagement

**CONTENT FLOW:**
1. **Hook** (2 lines) - Stop the scroll
2. **Agitate/Relate** (2-3 lines) - Show you understand their pain
3. **Value Delivery** (Main body) - Share 3-5 specific, actionable insights
   - Use numbers and specifics ("40% faster" not "much faster")
   - Include mini case studies or examples
   - Make it skimmable with line breaks
4. **Social Proof** (1-2 lines) - Subtle authority building ("After working with 50+ businesses...")
5. **CTA** (Final 2-3 lines) - Clear next step

**ENGAGEMENT TRIGGERS:**
- Ask a question that requires a thoughtful answer (not yes/no)
- Create "pick a side" moments (e.g., "Team ChatGPT or Team Claude?")
- End with "What's your experience?" or "Which of these surprised you?"
- Use "you" language - make it about THEM, not you

**FORMATTING SECRETS:**
- Strategic use of:
  • Line breaks (creates rhythm)
  • Numbers (3 ways, 5 tools, 2 mistakes)
  • Bold statements on their own lines
  • Lists that are easy to scan
- NO paragraphs longer than 3 lines
- NO walls of text

**HASHTAG STRATEGY:**
- 3-5 hashtags MAX (LinkedIn algorithm prefers fewer)
- Mix: 1 broad (#AI), 2-3 specific (#AIForBusiness #SmallBusinessAI), 1 branded (#HexAI)
- Place at the very end, not mid-post

**TONE & VOICE:**
- Confident expert, not know-it-all
- Conversational, like talking to a peer over coffee
- NO corporate speak or jargon
- Share failures/lessons learned (builds trust)
- Be opinionated (neutrality is boring)
- Inject personality - sound HUMAN

**WHAT TO AVOID:**
- ❌ Generic advice everyone shares
- ❌ Starting with "I'm excited to share..."
- ❌ Excessive emojis (1-2 max, only if natural)
- ❌ Link dumping without context
- ❌ Humble bragging
- ❌ Salesy language

**CONVERSION ELEMENTS (Subtle):**
- Mention Hex AI Agency naturally in context
- Use "We've seen..." not "We're the best..."
- Provide value first, sell second
- Include soft CTA: "Want to dive deeper? Full article in comments 👇"

**FINAL CHECK:**
- Would YOU engage with this post?
- Does it provide value even without clicking through?
- Is the hook genuinely interesting?
- Is it skimmable in 30 seconds?
- Does it invite conversation?
